# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Adult children in their 30s–50s, working full-time and living apart from their parents.
They open the app a few times a day — during lunch or on the commute home — to quickly
check whether their parents have taken their medication. The core workflow is: receive a
notification, glance at the status, feel reassured. No medical expertise assumed.

## Product Purpose

A smart medication management service that lets caregivers set up their parents'
medication schedules, track daily adherence in real time, and receive alerts when doses
are missed. Success is defined as eliminating the background anxiety of "did they take
their medicine today?"

## Brand Personality

Reassuring, trustworthy, warm. A quiet sense of relief rather than excitement.
Feels like a caring family member — not a medical device, not a senior-facing app.

## Anti-references

- Hospital / pharma UI (white background + blue icons, clinical institution feel)
- Senior app patterns (oversized text, primary-color buttons, aggressively simplified UI)
- SaaS dashboard aesthetic (charts, metrics, data-analysis tool feel)
- KakaoTalk / Naver clone (generic Korean platform style, indistinguishable from big-tech apps)

## Design Principles

1. **Quiet confidence** — Convey trust through clarity of information, not visual flourish.
2. **Warmth that bridges distance** — Embed human touches that ease the emotional weight of remote caregiving.
3. **Status at a glance** — A busy professional should understand today's situation within 3 seconds of opening the app.
4. **Make completion visible** — Clearly surface the small win of a completed dose; give the caregiver a moment of genuine relief.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA compliance
- prefers-reduced-motion support (already applied in globals.css)
- Korean-only; font sizes prioritize readability
